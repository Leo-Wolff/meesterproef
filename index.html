Ackaleth… a unknown continent that is definitely not within the world that I’m familiar with. I’ve been missing for days now, three to be exact.

Speaking of, welcome to day three of Adrien feeling like crap, it’s gonna be a bit of a theme for a while it seems.

I mean can you blame me? After three days of being here I have:

Almost fallen to my death, been threatened, had my soul leave my body apparently, found out I’m in a DIFFERENT WORLD, found out I’m surrounded by dying people, had my daydreams essentially remove a day from my life, and too much hiking.

Yaaay.

And no one, including me, had the slightest idea how I got transported here nor how to get back. How long would I be stuck here? If so much has happened within three days, what else awaited me?

Her younger brother and her had managed to get on their horses as they and four other guards rode beside them as a shield. They were galloping out of the city as soon as possible. Whatever had sieged her father’s kingdom, it sounded like they were winning and they were the only two who managed to get out on time.

Where the rest of her family was and if they survived, she was unsure.

I don’t want to see this.

All they could do was gallop at their fastest through the kingdom, hoping they will be able to cross the border in time to guarantee their own safety. Stella was taught to sit on her saddle with both legs on one side since that was more appropriate as a princess, and although it was a fine sitting position that still allowed Orion to run, once she realised Orion would have to jump over obstacles the situation seemed less than ideal.

She was forced to have her legs on either side by the time she saw what had happened to her castle.

A bright red light shot up from the highest tower, the sky becoming crimson as it expanded.

Let this scene just be over with, it’s too cruel.

The rest of her family was gone at this point, I knew that much. And I also understood no one else was going to make it. The group just kept running, their desperation increasing evermore until finally the first mistake happened. Her younger brother didn’t make the jump, he had fallen off his horse.

“KEEP GOING!” yelled one of the guards at Stella as she reluctantly continued her escape. All of the guards got off their horses and assisted the young prince but the delay proved fatal. The light had expanded so far out that it had reached them, Stella saw them all turn to stone.

Knowing her death was fast approaching she encouraged Orion to go faster. Her hand basically reaching out for something that was not even there. The symbolism didn’t pass me, I knew I myself was doing the same right now. Moreso, I needed to vent and I felt like this whole scene was just one big rant of my own crippling loneliness. Stella didn’t deserve it, but I couldn’t stop it either. This was her story, I somehow knew I couldn’t change that.

I felt ever more guilty as time started running out, the red light catching up as Orion galloped for dear life. Suddenly, she heard cawing and to her right saw a bright red bird she had never laid eyes on before in her life. The creature flew right besides her as the border approached ever closer.

Then, right before the dark spell would’ve hit Orion’s tail, they had crossed it. Stella wouldn’t have known that was the case if it wasn’t for the fact that the light for some reason did not wish to continue any further.

From the sudden shock of her escape, she unknowingly had pulled Orion’s reins. The force of the stop caused her to fall of her horse hard enough for her to pass out.

She laid there unconscious in the grass as the spell retreated. Her entire kingdom forever turned to stone. However, her phoenix had joined her side now, and a couple from the local village found her. It did not take long for them to figure out that Princess Stella was now the only member of her royal family left alive.

By the time I came to, it was already well past dinner time. The day had become a haze in my memory, spend either lying down or pacing about. I had no clue what it must’ve looked like for the campers. I didn’t care about that though.

If you have a daydreaming disorder, you know how consuming a binge like this can be. How it steals your time and life away from you until things are resolved. How it numbs your brain like a drug, and you can’t get off the high until you are satisfied. Much like a drug… it was a great feeling, and lethally addictive.

When I mean lethal, I sort of mean it. Because when I look back on my life, it was only a half-lived one. One life living. One life wandering.

In all honesty reader, living a half-life hurts, a lot. Breathing hurts, a lot. Existing hurts, a lot. As if everything was a suffocating gas that I was forced to take in. That pain has been with me for what feels like forever, although I have somewhat grown numb to it.

That is why I knew I would never be done wanting to drift off. It was a life sentence. However, for now, I was satisfied. I had compensated my lost daydream time from yesterday.

This can’t happen again though.

I had to reaffirm myself of that. I was in a camp full of people with a terminal illness that may or may not be contagious, on a mountain I could not navigate alone, in a continent that is completely unknown to me. And seeing as how so many of the campers appeared to have scarring, I couldn’t afford to be too ignorant.

If Cyprian said my daydreams could be useful – which would be the very first time that was a thing – then I had to focus on giving them practical use. What I did today was a waste of time, it was leisure.

“You awake?” I looked at the source of the voice. I had recognised it; it was the same American accented voice from yesterday. I also remembered this voice heavily objected to my being here, but that’s when he had assumed I was Kethterran. This time, I could finally put a face to the voice. He had most of the usual traits that the celekaine seemed to possess here; fur attire, scars, and celekaine markings. However, he seemed on the older side of the people here, which wasn’t saying a lot. Estelle was very much the odd person out by being a senior, everyone else seemed to either be in their teens or twenties.

The fact that all of them were dying was a depressing thought.

This guy had his markings right within his right eye. The whole thing was matt black, only a small outline of white still remained. The disease was clearly trying to take over the entire thing and then continue its vicious hunt for the rest of the body. His left eye showed what his right eye should’ve been, a soft blue shade.

I nodded and he handed me a plate of food that mainly consisted of berries and meat.

“Thank you.” I said awkwardly. He furrowed his brows and his piercing gaze felt like daggers.

“It’s fine.” He replied with no emotion whatsoever. I attempted a conversation by saying,

“I’m not Kethterran you know…” He looked at me for a moment seemingly in thought.

“I know.” I didn’t know what to say to that, so an uncomfortable silence ensued. Great.

He offered this time to continue the chat by saying, “is that how people of your world normally rest?”

“Huh?” He elaborated,

“The pacing.”

“Oh… no that’s… that’s a me thing.” I didn’t want to tell him about my daydreaming disorder or the daydreams themselves. Estelle and Cyprian knowing was about as much as I wanted to share. However, he spotted my reluctance and his emotionless display revealed to have hidden frustration.

“Okay then. Anyways, enjoy your meal. Alexandra worked hard on it.” I ignored his shift in tone. I simply nodded and replied once again,

“Thank you.” I thought the conversation was over but the guy kept looking at me. I could’ve sworn that the look had a hint of pity within it. “What is it?” I asked. He shook his head in response before walking away,

“Nothing… Nothing.”

After dinner by the campfire I had heard from a woman, who introduced herself as Atiya, that my new clothes had been finished. Apparently the man who handed me my dinner, Markus, was supposed to tell me that they were done. It did not surprise me why he didn’t. He didn’t seem to like me very much.

I couldn’t really blame him though. It sounded like Kethterrans were a real threat and if they thought I was one at first… well it wasn’t a great start to my stay here. Never mind the whole ‘I’m from a whole other universe/dimension/world/whatever’. I collected my new fur attire and immediately realised that these clothes have a getting dressed process to them that I have not been informed about.

Fortunately, Kamitsu had just entered the tent and saw my desperate look. Thus she instructed me and let me get dressed privately in her tent that she shared with Sharaf.

Her tent was a goldish one, one of the few ones that didn’t seem to match with any of the others in terms of style. However, the inside was a lot more modest and was mainly filled with books. The books got me excited. They weren’t the kind of covers you normally buy at a modern bookstore. These books were leather-bound, that classic book look.

You’re a reader, you understand the hype. You want to smell it, I do too. We’re on the same page… pun intended.

As I was getting dressed Kamitsu said from outside the tent,

“Feeling rested?”

“Not really.” I replied with an exhausted chuckle.

“Really? You didn’t seem to be doing a lot.” I didn’t reply. Once again, I didn’t want to open up about this side of me. It felt too personal. “Does it have to do with the task Cyprian mentioned?”

“Kind of.” Was all that I was willing to say about it. It was silent for a good while. Despite Kamitsu’s instructions I was still struggling a fair bit with the clothing.

“So before Cyprian left he suggested it could be a good idea to have you hike up to the hot springs. I was thinking… you could do that tomorrow. The springs are a spiritual area. I don’t know what you have to do but he said it could help.”

“Maybe… I’m not sure.” My heart was pounding for some reason. I think it was a mix of getting dressed whilst having a conversation with a person I had only known for two days, and the subject matter itself.

“I’ll ask who wants to accompany you, it’s not far. It’s a half day’s climb at most.” I was dressed at this point, but another question had been burning on my mind ever since this morning.

“I hope I’m not a burden… am I?” There was a silence before Kamitsu responded,

“You’re just inexperienced, that’s all.” I appreciated the directness, but the truth still stung somewhat. My silence must’ve motivated Kamitsu to make an attempt to cheer me up, “we all had to adjust to this life too, I wouldn’t worry about it.”

Could I adjust? Did I want to? It seemed the longer I was here the more questions I got.
