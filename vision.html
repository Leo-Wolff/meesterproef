My Vision

Maladaptive daydreaming disorder isn’t a commonly known disorder yet still people struggle with it. People with the disorder struggle balancing between reality and fantasy and sometimes they even blend together. It can be a disorientating disorder and since the maladaptive daydreaming isn’t within the DSM-5 people can’t get a medical diagnosis for it, which in turns means lack of proper treatment.

Daydreams are often seen as a positive experience, being able to daydream a whole storyline even more so. However, this website aims to show that “too much of a good thing” counts for daydreaming too. Some of the symptoms of the disorder include:

  • Disassociating
  • Struggles with executive function
  • Pacing or mouth movements lined up with your daydreaming
  • Daydreaming for an excessive amount of time (hours)

If you think you mean have the disorder know that you are not alone and much like riding a bike in time things will get easier.
